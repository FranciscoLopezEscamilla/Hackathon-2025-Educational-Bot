The CONCACAF Champions League is an annual international club football tournament run by CONCACAF, the sport's governing body for North and Central America and the Caribbean. The tournament was established in 1962 and was renamed the CONCACAF Champions League in 2008. The competition is open to all eligible clubs in the region, and the winner earns a spot in the FIFA Club World Cup. The tournament typically features a group stage, followed by a knockout stage, with the final match determining the champion. The competition has been won by a number of prominent clubs in the region, including CF Monterrey, Club América, and Los Angeles Galaxy. The CONCACAF Champions League has been considered one of the most prestigious club competitions in the region, and it has attracted top teams from the United States, Mexico, and other countries in the region. However, in recent years, the tournament has faced some challenges, including a decline in participation and a lack of investment from some of the participating clubs.
